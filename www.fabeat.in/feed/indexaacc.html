    Nothing restores and repairs your body and mind like a long, sound sleep can. Apart from its healing abilities, good sleep means balanced hormones which in turn promises a well functioning body.

16/8 method

The most commonly adopted intermittent fasting style, here your eating window is restricted to 8 hours and you shall be fasting for the remaining 16 hours in a day. For example, you can skip breakfast and break your fast for the day for lunch, say 2 pm and then have dinner at 10pm. Make sure you don’t give in to any cravings during the fasting hours. 

5:2 method

Easier of the three to start with, in the 5:2 method you eat like how you would normally do on five days of the week and then cut down the calories drastically to 500 on the remaining two days. Ideally alternate these two days rather than combining them for an easier transition. 

24h method

Also known commonly as the eat-stop-eat method, here you eat like normal 6 days a week and fast for an entire day. If you’re feeling it, you can switch up to fasting for two days, spacing the days alternatively. Harder of the three to follow, this is a method only seasoned fasting practitioners can use. 

Now that you are familiar with all the three methods of fasting, do your research and pick a style that suits you and stick to it to see the amazing benefits of intermittent fasting to your mind and body.

With the ongoing COVID-19 issue, strengthening our immunity system is the only way to keep the virus at bay and be confident enough to overcome the viral infection even if you are affected by it. Here are our top five recommendations that are really easy to prepare and yummy to drink when it comes to liquids you can consume to boost your immunity. 

Spicy lemonade

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups water
  • Lemon juice of one medium sized lemon
  • Few gratings of ginger
  • 2-3  chopped garlic cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Few mint leaves
  • 1-2 tbsp. organic honey, to taste

Method:

Add all the ingredients except honey to water and bring to a boil. Strain the mixture and add honey and consume warm. 

Spiced tea

Ingredients:

  • Peppercorns – 2-3
  • Cloves – 2-3
  • Ginger grated – 1 tbsp
  • Cinnamon – 1stick
  • Crushed cardamom – 1-2
  • Star anise – 1
  • Few tulsi leaves
  • Honey to taste

Method:

Boil all the ingredients together for 25-30 minutes without honey. Strain and consume lukewarm with honey. 

Spicy turmeric-milk drink

Ingredients:

  • 1 tsp freshly grated turmeric
  • ½ tsp grated ginger
  • 1.5-2 cups of soy/almond milk
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  •  1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 tbsp honey

Method:

Lightly heat all the ingredients except honey for 8-10 minutes without bringing to boil. Strain and serve warm adding the required amount of honey. 

Green smoothie

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of baby spinach
  • ½ cup of cucumber
  • 1 cup of curds
  •  ½ tsp lemon juice
  • 1-2 cups ice cubes
  • Few mint leaves
  • Salt to taste

Method:

Blend all the ingredients together until smooth and consume immediately. 

Turmeric-Ginger chai

Ingredients:

  • Water- 3 cups
  • Fresh turmeric grated 1-2 tsp
  • Grated ginger- 1 tsp
  • Lemon juice 1 tbsp
  • Honey to taste

Method:

Boil turmeric and ginger in water for 12-15 minutes, strain and add lemon juice. Serve warm with honey.

]]> Being in the midst of a global pandemic that’s spreading at an alarming rate, building our immunity is the only sure shot way to stay safe and protected. Let’s take a look at what is responsible for having a strong immunity and the ways and means to achieve it today. 

Getting the right amount of sleep

Sound sleep is the most important thing when it comes to building your immunity. As you might be aware, the body heals itself during sleep. So getting at least 6-8 hours of undisturbed sleep, punctually is vital. 

Plenty and plenty of water

Rightly known as the elixir of life, water is essential to not just quench your thirst alone and cleanse your system. Water ensures the proper circulation of electrolytes in the body, nutrient absorption and flushing out the toxins. Drink at least eight glasses of water everyday to maintain the right fluid levels in your body. 

Green veggies and colourful fruits 

The right supply of vitamins, proteins and fibres are quintessential in building your immune system and for the overall development of your body. Apart from fruits and vegetables, nuts, seeds, carbs, good fats all play an equally important role. Fermented foods having good bacteria that aid digestion should also be consumed for one’s gut health. 

Exercise

It goes without saying that exercise and robust activity is essential for holding up the physical and mental health of your body. Exercise promotes good metabolism, flexibility and keeps the mind and body stress free. This in turn urges the improvement of the immune system and aids in fighting the foreign invaders to the body. 

Flushing out toxins

Overtime, toxins accumulate in the body and it has to be regularly cleaned out. Routine fasting, going easy on heavy meals and consuming drinks rich in antioxidants like green tea and lemon helps flush out the toxic substances and harmful bacteria and boost your immune system.
