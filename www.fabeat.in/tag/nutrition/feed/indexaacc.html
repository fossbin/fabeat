The most essential micronutrient to keep your body functioning smoothly, vitamins come in several types and required doses. While some can be achieved through the food we consume , some have to be supplied to the body externally. Either way, let’s take a look at five of the most vital vitamins essential to your body today and the ways you can incorporate them in your diet.

VITAMIN A

Primarily known as the most required vitamin for eyesight, Vitamin A is also required for your immunity, reproduction and the overall cell development. 

Sources of Vitamin A includes:
Fish oils
Milk
Eggs
Carrots
Green leafy vegetables

VITAMIN B12

Commonly called cobalamin, Vitamin B12 helps balance hormones, ensure proper functioning of the nervous system and keep stress at bay. It is essential for the production of the red blood cells as well. 

Sources of Vitamin B12 includes:

Meats, poultry and eggs

Dairy products like milk, curd and cheese

VITAMIN C 

Ascorbic acid rich Vitamin C is your repairman when it comes to the wear and tear issues of your cells. It promotes wound healing and the proper building of the bones. It is essential in building immunity as well. 

Sources of Vitamin C includes:

Citrus fruits like lemon, oranges etc

Green leafy veggies

Potatoes

VITAMIN D

The building factor behind strong bones and teeth, Vitamin D is important for muscle building as well. This is one vitamin that cannot be consumed through foods alone. Ample sunshine is the only way one can ensure this essential vitamin is absorbed in the body. 

Sources of Vitamin D includes:

Fish oil

Eggs

Soya

VITAMIN K

Responsible for controlling the blood pressure and blood coagulation, Vitamin K is indispensable when it comes to wound repair and healing.

Sources of Vitamin K includes:

Nuts and seeds

Diary and eggs

Meat

Soya

That’s all folks! Make sure you are consuming the above mentioned vitamin sources to ensure proper functioning of your body, at all times.

Diet is really important, and I think vitamins are really important, also.

Glenn Danzing

]]> Being in the midst of a global pandemic that’s spreading at an alarming rate, building our immunity is the only sure shot way to stay safe and protected. Let’s take a look at what is responsible for having a strong immunity and the ways and means to achieve it today. 

Getting the right amount of sleep

Sound sleep is the most important thing when it comes to building your immunity. As you might be aware, the body heals itself during sleep. So getting at least 6-8 hours of undisturbed sleep, punctually is vital. 

Plenty and plenty of water

Rightly known as the elixir of life, water is essential to not just quench your thirst alone and cleanse your system. Water ensures the proper circulation of electrolytes in the body, nutrient absorption and flushing out the toxins. Drink at least eight glasses of water everyday to maintain the right fluid levels in your body. 

Green veggies and colourful fruits 

The right supply of vitamins, proteins and fibres are quintessential in building your immune system and for the overall development of your body. Apart from fruits and vegetables, nuts, seeds, carbs, good fats all play an equally important role. Fermented foods having good bacteria that aid digestion should also be consumed for one’s gut health. 

Exercise

It goes without saying that exercise and robust activity is essential for holding up the physical and mental health of your body. Exercise promotes good metabolism, flexibility and keeps the mind and body stress free. This in turn urges the improvement of the immune system and aids in fighting the foreign invaders to the body. 

Flushing out toxins

Overtime, toxins accumulate in the body and it has to be regularly cleaned out. Routine fasting, going easy on heavy meals and consuming drinks rich in antioxidants like green tea and lemon helps flush out the toxic substances and harmful bacteria and boost your immune system. 

Thus, along with proper sanitizing and washing of hands, make sure you follow these tips to upgrade your body’s fighting mechanism and together let’s combat this epidemic. 

Take care of your body that’s the only place you have to live.

Jim Rohn
